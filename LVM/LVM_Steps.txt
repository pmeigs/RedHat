I have a volume group named rhel. Within that group I have two mounted volumes /var and /var/log. I want to extend /var and /var/log to a single hard drive that has two partitions /dev/vdd1 and /dev/vdd2
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
sudo fdisk /dev/vdb
create 2 paritions vdb1 vdb2
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Before making changes, confirm the state of your volume group and logical volumes:

sudo vgdisplay rhel
sudo lvdisplay rhel/var
sudo lvdisplay rhel/var_log
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Check if the partitions are already LVM physical volumes:

sudo pvdisplay /dev/vdb1
sudo pvdisplay /dev/vdb2

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
If they are not already LVM physical volumes, initialize them:

sudo pvcreate /dev/vdb1
sudo pvcreate /dev/vdb2

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Extend the rhel volume group to include the new physical volumes:

sudo vgextend rhel /dev/vdb1 /dev/vdb2

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Verify the volume group now includes the new partitions:

Check the “Free PE / Size” field to confirm the added capacity.

vgdisplay rhel

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OPTIONAL:
Extend /var (e.g., add 50% of the available space or a specific size):

lvextend -L +10G rhel/var

Adjust the size as needed. To use all available space in the volume group for one logical volume, use:

lvextend -l +100%FREE rhel/var

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Alternatively, if you want to assign specific partitions:

lvextend rhel/var /dev/vdb1       <-------equivalent to /dev/rhel/var
lvextend rhel/var_log /dev/vdb2   <-------equivalent to /dev/rhel/var_log

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Resize the File Systems:

For ext4 filesystems:

resize2fs /dev/rhel/var
resize2fs /dev/rhel/var/log

For XFS filesystems:

xfs_growfs /var
xfs_growfs /var/log
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Resize cryptsetup LUKS:

cryptsetup resize <crypt-id-number> (i.e.: luks-8825ae53-dbd2-459b-9cd9-1eb5dcfcaa89)

sudo cryptsetup resize luks-453d894a-bcbf-49fb-8130-930b5a7e0eb3 (example for var)
sudo cryptsetup resize luks-d38a38e3-8759-4428-87d8-ba463e39e74f (example for var_log)
